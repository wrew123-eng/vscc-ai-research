5 TLDR Bullets

  • DeepSeek inflection point: The Jan 2025 release of DeepSeek-R1 demonstrated China can produce frontier-class AI at drastically lower compute cost, forcing a global reassessment of the US–China AI gap and triggering a "Crossroads Era" in Chinese policy (Carnegie Endowment, Jul 2025).
  • Massive state capital mobilisation: China's top internet firms are targeting >$70B in AI data-centre capex in 2025 alone; the Bank of China's "AI Industry Development Action Plan" pledges ¥1 trillion ($137B) over five years to AI infrastructure and applications (Bank of China, Mar 2025).
  • Sovereign hardware stack taking shape: Huawei Ascend 910C is now the de-facto domestic GPU standard; SMIC is scaling 7nm-class DUV production and targeting 5nm-class nodes, creating a tariff-proof domestic AI supply chain — albeit still 1–2 generations behind TSMC/Nvidia (Tom's Hardware, 2025).
  • Comprehensive policy architecture: The State Council's "AI+ Action Plan" (Aug 2025) sets legally binding sector-penetration targets of 70% by 2027 and 90% by 2030 across six priority domains, backed by MIIT draft ethics rules and mandatory AI content-labelling regulations effective Sep 2025.
  • Military-civilian fusion is accelerating: The PLA has adopted DeepSeek LLMs for intelligence analysis and is advancing toward AI-enabled battlefield C2 (command & control), with mid-2030s full "intelligentised warfare" as the declared goal (DoD China Military Power Report, Dec 2025).

AI Maturity Rating

4.0 / 5 — China has credible frontier-class AI model capability, a scaling sovereign hardware stack, strong state funding discipline, and a clear regulatory framework. The remaining gap is in leading-edge chip manufacturing (<5nm at scale), EDA tooling, and raw compute density vs. the US. Not a laggard — a credible challenger.

Investor Watch The inflection is no longer theoretical. China's AI trajectory is investment-relevant now, not in 2030. The key question is whether domestic compute supply can scale fast enough to match state-mandated deployment targets.

National AI Vision & Goals

The Three-Phase Blueprint (2017–2030)

China's overarching AI strategy is anchored in the 新一代人工智能发展规划 (New Generation AI Development Plan, NGAIDP), issued by the State Council in July 2017. It set a three-phase roadmap:

Phase 12020Align with global leaders; key applications world-classAI core industry >¥150B; related industries >¥1T
Phase 22025Major breakthroughs in AI basic theory; become main driver of industrial upgradingAI core industry >¥400B (~$56B); related industries >¥5T (~$690B)
Phase 32030Global AI innovation centre; AI industry world-leadingAI core industry >¥1T (~$138B); total AI economy >¥10T (~$1.4T)

Source: State Council NGAIDP (Jul 2017); OECD.AI Policy Dashboard

2025 Phase 2 Status Check

Phase 2 (2025) milestones were partially met but with important caveats:

  • Model capability: Achieved — DeepSeek-R1 (Jan 2025) matches GPT-4 class on key benchmarks at <10% of training cost.
  • Industry size: Roughly on track — China AI market estimated at ¥580B+ in 2024; core AI industries approaching ¥400B target.
  • Theory/research: Mixed — China is top-2 in AI publications (Stanford HAI, 2025 AI Index) but still trails the US in foundational breakthroughs.
  • Governance framework: Substantially achieved — Generative AI rules (Aug 2023), AI content labelling (Sep 2025), and draft AI ethics measures (Aug 2025) all in place.
  • Chip self-sufficiency: Not achieved — still dependent on legacy NVIDIA H800/H20 stockpiles; Huawei Ascend is functional but at roughly 60–70% of NVIDIA H100 throughput for most workloads.

AI+ Action Plan 2025–2035 (人工智能+行动计划)

Released by the State Council on 27 Aug 2025, the "AI+ Action Plan" is the operative successor to the NGAIDP. It is China's current national AI blueprint. Key declared targets:

  • By 2027: AI penetration >70% across six priority domains; >70% of users using AI-integrated smart devices and intelligent agents.
  • By 2030: AI penetration >90%; "intelligent economy" to be a primary growth driver; China to be a global AI governance standard-setter.
  • By 2035: Full "intelligentised society"; AI to contribute an estimated 26% of GDP growth (government projection).
Key Signal for Investors The shift from NGAIDP (aspiration) to AI+ Action Plan (mandated sector targets with enforcement mechanisms) marks a qualitative escalation in policy seriousness. Sector-penetration quotas backed by state procurement = guaranteed revenue streams for compliant domestic vendors.

International Strategy

Digital Silk Road 2.0 (数字䮐综之路)

China's international AI strategy operates through three overlapping channels: infrastructure export via the Digital Silk Road (DSR), governance norm-setting at multilateral forums, and corporate expansion via Huawei/Alibaba/ByteDance.

Belt & Road AI Infrastructure Footprint

  • Africa: Huawei Cloud and Alibaba Cloud have expanded data-centre infrastructure across Kenya, Nigeria, South Africa, Egypt, and Ethiopia. Huawei supports school digitalisation and rural connectivity in Rwanda, Uganda, and Cameroon — creating AI dependency pathways (East Asia Forum, Apr 2025).
  • Southeast Asia: ByteDance, Alibaba, and Tencent are the dominant cloud providers in Indonesia, Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. Alibaba Cloud's Singapore hub serves as the regional AI gateway. Singapore is the primary transit node for Chinese AI services reaching global markets.
  • Central Asia: Post-Ukraine, Central Asian states (Kazakhstan, Uzbekistan, Kyrgyzstan) are deepening Chinese AI partnerships as US/EU tech access becomes politically contested (China-Global South Project, 2025).
  • Middle East: Saudi Aramco and UAE's G42 have partnered with Chinese AI firms; Huawei is building AI cloud infrastructure in Saudi Arabia and the UAE.

Governance Norm-Setting

  • At the Paris AI Summit (Feb 2025), China signed the "Inclusive AI" declaration while the US declined — a diplomatic win that framed China as the inclusive AI multilateralist vs. US unilateralism.
  • China released its Global AI Governance Action Plan (全球人工智能治理行动计划) in July 2025 via the UN Mission, proposing a UN-led multilateral AI governance body and pushing "technology sovereignty" as a framing that resonates with Global South states.
  • China is actively exporting AI governance templates to BRI countries — replicating its own regulatory model (state oversight, content control, data localisation) as the default for less-developed AI regulatory environments.

Bottom Line on Risks The chip gap is the single most consequential constraint. Everything else — talent, data, regulation — China has workable mitigation paths. The compute ceiling imposed by EDA + EUV restrictions is the structural limitation that cannot be fully domesticated before 2030 at the earliest. This is why DeepSeek's efficiency innovation matters so much: it makes China competitive at lower compute density.

Key Takeaways for Investors
Investment Stance China AI is not uninvestable — but it is a different risk-return profile from US AI. The upside is state-guaranteed procurement scale and structural domestic market advantages. The downside is geopolitical tail risk, governance uncertainty, and a compute ceiling that will periodically bite.

5 Actionable Investment Bullets

  1. Back the sovereign hardware stack, not just models. The most durable investment thesis in China AI is the domestic chip ecosystem — Huawei Ascend, SMIC advanced nodes, and the 60+ Hubble-backed semiconductor companies. Every US export-control tightening is a free tailwind for this trade. Relevant listed proxy: SMIC (688981.SS, 0981.HK). The Ascend ecosystem is not yet public but watch for IPOs in the Hubble portfolio 2026–2028.
  2. Lean into the AI+ procurement mandate in healthcare and industrial AI. The State Council's 70%-by-2027 sector penetration target means government and SOE procurement budgets are legally committed. Companies supplying AI tools to hospitals (United Imaging Healthcare, 688585.SS) and smart manufacturing platforms (iSoftStone, 301236.SZ; Supcon Technology, 688777.SS) have near-captive near-term revenue. These are not speculative — contracts are being signed now.
  3. Treat DeepSeek-class efficiency as a structural thesis, not a one-off event. China's approach of open-sourcing competitive frontier models at low cost is a deliberate strategic posture (USCC, Mar 2026). This compresses AI margins globally, disadvantages closed-model Western incumbents in emerging markets, and accelerates China's standard-setting role. For global AI portfolios, this means avoiding over-concentration in closed-model API businesses and considering infrastructure (compute, data, deployment tooling) that benefits regardless of which model wins.
  4. Monitor the Digital Silk Road as an early-cycle AI infrastructure investment signal. Chinese cloud and AI companies (Alibaba Cloud, Huawei Cloud, ByteDance) are aggressively building data-centre capacity across SEA, Middle East, and Africa. These are pre-competitive moves establishing standards and switching costs. Singapore-listed infrastructure REITs and regional data-centre operators (e.g. Keppel DC REIT, KDCREIT.SI) benefit from Chinese and US hyperscaler demand simultaneously — a rare non-zero-sum position.
  5. Size the geopolitical tail risk explicitly and hedge it. A Taiwan conflict or major semiconductor sanctions escalation would be a catastrophic event for China AI positions. Scenario: 2–3% portfolio allocation to out-of-the-money put options on Chinese tech ETFs (KWEB, CQQQ) as insurance, reviewed quarterly against PLA posture signals. The base case (continued managed competition, no hot conflict) supports a constructive China AI view through 2027 — but the tail is fat enough to warrant explicit hedging, not just mental noting.
Timing Horizon 12–18 months (2026–mid-2027): AI+ procurement contracts accelerating; Huawei Ascend 910C adoption expanding; SMIC 7nm yield improvements should be visible in quarterly reports.

3–5 years (2027–2030): Domestic AI chip stack approaching commercial viability at scale; AI+ sector-penetration targets (70% → 90%) create secular demand; Digital Silk Road creates global market position difficult to displace.

10-year horizon (2030–2035): Outcome is binary — either China closes the compute gap and achieves genuine AI parity with the US, or structural chip constraints cap China's AI ceiling. The policy and investment signals for which scenario is materialising will be visible by 2028.
