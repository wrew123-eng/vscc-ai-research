Why Nano? The Logic of Supplier Investing

The Macro stage identified the structural forces (US–China AI race, physical AI, sovereign infrastructure). The Micro stage named 18 specific beneficiaries across Compute, Foundation Models, and Autonomous/Physical AI. Nano goes one level deeper: who do all 18 of those companies depend on?

The method is a supply chain intersection analysis. For each of the 18 Micro companies, we map backwards to identify critical inputs — packaging, EDA tools, power, sensors, memory, and data infrastructure — then cluster companies where the customer base overlaps across multiple Micro themes. The result is 6 clusters of non-obvious, high-conviction enablers.

Why prefer these over headline names? Three reasons: (1) Enablers capture AI capex regardless of which GPU or model wins. (2) They face less valuation competition from retail flows — most are not "AI stocks" in public narrative. (3) Switching costs and supply constraints create durable pricing power that pure software or chip names rarely achieve at scale.

C1
Advanced Semiconductor Packaging
Theme A · Theme B Linkage

The unsung bottleneck of the AI hardware stack — every NVDA Blackwell, AMD MI300X, and Cambricon chip needs advanced 2.5D/3D packaging to physically connect GPU dies to HBM memory stacks at ultra-high bandwidth. CoWoS capacity at TSMC is fully booked through 2026. OSATs (Outsourced Semiconductor Assembly and Test) are the relief valve — and they are printing money.

Why High-Potential

  • Hard physical constraint: Advanced packaging requires specialised equipment (lithography, thermal compression bonding) and multi-year factory buildouts. No shortcut exists.
  • Pricing power: TSMC CoWoS-L/S reportedly fully booked; OSAT partners such as ASE commanding premium ASPs for CoWoP solutions.
  • Customer lock-in: Packaging is co-designed with chip architecture — switching mid-generation is not feasible.
  • Cross-theme demand: AI accelerators (Theme A), hyperscaler custom silicon (Theme B), and automotive AI chips (Theme C) all require advanced packaging.

EDA Software & Chip IP
Theme A Linkage · All 6 Chip Companies

Every chip that NVDA, AMD, AVGO, Cambricon, Haiguang, and SMIC design passes through EDA (Electronic Design Automation) tools and licenced IP blocks before a single wafer is processed. The EDA duopoly of Synopsys and Cadence, plus Arm's CPU/NPU IP, form an absolute chokepoint in the AI chip supply chain. Switching costs are effectively infinite — chip designs are built inside these tools over multi-year development cycles.

Why High-Potential

  • Near-infinite switching costs: Entire design teams, PDKs, and IP libraries are embedded in EDA vendor environments. Re-qualification alone takes 2–4 years.
  • Revenue visibility: Both SNPS and CDNS operate on 3-year backlog contracts. SNPS exited FY2025 with a record US$11.4B backlog.
  • AI multiplier within EDA: AI chips are more complex (chiplet designs, 3D integration) and require more EDA hours per tapeout than previous generations.
  • Arm royalty leverage: Every additional Arm-based AI chip sold generates automatic royalty revenue — pure operating leverage with no incremental cost.

The world's #1 EDA company by revenue. FY2025 revenue of US$7.054B (+15% YoY). Completed the Ansys acquisition (US$35B), adding simulation software and transforming SNPS into a broader engineering design platform. Nearly 5,000 active AI-assisted design users among Tier-1 semiconductor customers.

AI Tailwind — Why Now

  • Design Automation revenue grew 65% YoY in Q4 FY2025, reflecting surging demand as AI chipmakers accelerate tapeout cycles.
  • Record backlog of US$11.4B — 41% higher than a year earlier. This is 18 months of forward revenue visibility. Most software companies would kill for this.
  • FY2026 guidance of US$9.61B (mid-point) includes US$2.9B Ansys contribution. Combined SNPS+Ansys creates the most comprehensive chip-to-system design platform — essential for AI factory buildouts.

Key Risk

China revenue declined 18% in FY2025 due to export controls — this is a real and ongoing headwind. At ~17–18% of prior revenue, China exposure is material. Premium valuation (~40x forward earnings) leaves limited margin for error on growth deceleration.

Same China exposure risk as Synopsys. Valuation is demanding but not quite as stretched as SNPS. The bigger risk is if AI chip complexity plateaus — Cadence's revenue growth is highly correlated to the number and complexity of new AI chip tapeouts per year.

Designs the CPU, GPU, and NPU IP that is licensed to virtually every chip company globally. Arm-based chips power Apple Silicon, Qualcomm Snapdragon, AWS Graviton, Google TPUs, and increasingly AI edge devices. Revenue model: licence fees upfront + royalties per chip shipped.

The stock is not cheap — it is a quality bet on the long-term dominance of Arm architecture in AI, which is high-probability but priced for perfection near-term.

AI Data Centre Power & Cooling
Theme B Linkage · All 6 Foundation Model Companies

Every GPU cluster needs power delivery and thermal management before it can turn on. MSFT, GOOGL, META, Baidu, Alibaba, and Tencent are collectively committing over US$300B to data centre capex in 2025–2026. The physical infrastructure layer — power conversion, uninterruptible power supply, liquid cooling, and high-speed networking — is an underappreciated beneficiary that grows proportionally with every dollar of hyperscaler spend.

Why High-Potential

  • Proportional capex capture: For every US$1 of GPU spend, approximately US$0.50–1.00 is spent on power and cooling. This ratio rises with AI rack densities (now 100–400kW per rack vs 10kW previously).
  • Liquid cooling inflection: Air cooling reaches physical limits above ~50kW per rack. Liquid cooling is no longer optional for AI clusters — it is mandatory. This creates a new product cycle for incumbents.
  • Long backlog visibility: Vertiv's backlog reached US$9.5B (+30% YoY); Eaton's total backlog hit record US$19.6B. These are 12–24 month visibility windows.
  • Network bandwidth as constraint: AI training and inference require ultra-low-latency, ultra-high-bandwidth network fabrics — Arista Networks is the de facto standard for AI data centre spine networking.

Background

The leading provider of power and cooling infrastructure for data centres — UPS systems, precision cooling, power distribution units, and thermal management. Revenue scale: ~US$10B+ (FY2025 guidance raised to US$10.16–10.24B). Serves all major hyperscalers globally.

AI Tailwind — Why Now

  • Q3 2025 revenue of US$2.676B, up 29% YoY. Americas segment grew 43%. Organic orders grew approximately 60% YoY in Q3 2025 — this is demand backlog building, not yet revenue.
  • Backlog of US$9.5B with a book-to-bill ratio of ~1.4x — every dollar of revenue recognised is being replaced by 1.4x new orders. Revenue acceleration is the base case.
  • Liquid cooling product line directly addresses the GPU rack density problem. Vertiv's CEO explicitly called the liquid-cooling inflection "accelerating and evolving" for AI workloads specifically.

Key Risk

Vertiv's revenue is highly concentrated in hyperscaler customers. Any pause or capex cycle reversal from the top 5 hyperscalers would crater orders quickly. Supply chain execution risk on scaling liquid cooling manufacturing — a new product category at volume.

Precision Sensors & LiDAR
Theme C Linkage · Physical AI Enablers

Physical AI — autonomous vehicles, industrial robots, surgical systems — cannot exist without perception. Sensors are the eyes and ears of every robot, EV, autonomous vehicle, and smart factory. This cluster spans machine vision (Cognex, Keyence) for industrial automation and LiDAR (Hesai) for automotive and robotics — the lowest-level physical enablers of the physical AI megatrend.

Why High-Potential

  • Non-substitutable hardware: Software models need sensor data to learn and act. No sensor = no physical AI. Unlike pure software, sensor vendors cannot be disrupted by a model upgrade.
  • Volume leverage: As TSLA, BYD, ESTUN, and Hesai (as customer) scale production, sensor attachment rates per vehicle/robot generate compounding volume growth.
  • Industrial AI retrofit cycle: Factories globally are adding machine vision to quality control and robotics integration — a multi-decade retrofit opportunity.
  • China LiDAR dominance: Hesai is the world's first profitable LiDAR company, with Chinese OEM relationships unmatched by Western peers.

HBM & High-Bandwidth Memory
Theme A · Theme B Linkage

High Bandwidth Memory (HBM) is the most critical constraint in the AI accelerator supply chain after TSMC packaging. Every NVDA H200/B200 and AMD MI300X integrates HBM3E stacks — without them, the GPU cannot achieve the memory bandwidth needed for large AI models. The HBM market is an effective triopoly: SK Hynix dominant, Micron ramping aggressively, Samsung qualifying. Pricing power is extreme.

Why High-Potential

  • Artificial scarcity: HBM requires 3D stacking technology (TSV — Through-Silicon Via) developed over a decade. New entrants are effectively impossible in any 5-year horizon.
  • Price discipline: SK Hynix and Samsung are reportedly planning a 20% price hike for 2026 HBM3E supply — a sign of confident pricing power in a seller's market.
  • TAM explosion: HBM TAM for calendar 2025 exceeds US$35B, and demand is growing faster than supply. Every new AI accelerator generation uses more HBM stacks per chip.
  • Micron's underdog opportunity: Micron's share was ~21% in mid-2025 (vs SK Hynix 62%) — significant catch-up potential as HBM3E 12hi production ramps, with multibillion-dollar HBM revenue now confirmed.
SK Hynix 000660 · Korea Exchange

Background

The world's dominant HBM supplier. Revenue scale: ~US$66B+ in FY2025 (total memory). NVIDIA drives approximately 27% of SK Hynix total revenue — an extraordinary single-customer concentration that is also extraordinary business quality. HBM3E market share: 62% in Q2 2025, 57% in Q3 2025. Remains NVIDIA's primary HBM supplier for Blackwell and Rubin platforms.

AI Tailwind — Why Now

  • NVIDIA drove ~₩11 trillion in H1 2025 revenue from SK Hynix alone. SK Hynix sold out its entire DRAM, NAND, and HBM supply to Nvidia through 2026 — there is literally no spare capacity. This is the most demand-certain supply arrangement in semiconductors.
  • HBM3E to remain flagship through 2026 (accounting for ~two-thirds of HBM shipments). SK Hynix's HBM4 samples delivered to NVIDIA, with Rubin GPU integration on track — the customer relationship is multi-generational.
  • Overtook Samsung as world's largest memory chip supplier by revenue in 2025 — a historic first, driven entirely by HBM market leadership.

Key Risk

Samsung is closing the HBM4 gap. If Samsung completes NVIDIA qualification (HBM4 samples delivered, expected contracts in 2026), SK Hynix's share will compress from 62% towards 50%. The Korea Exchange listing also means FX risk (KRW) and less familiar accounting for non-Korean investors.

HBM is structurally tighter than DRAM/NAND, but if AI chip volume expectations disappoint, HBM demand softens and pricing discipline breaks. Micron also remains #3 in HBM behind SK Hynix and potentially Samsung.

Samsung Electronics 005930 · Korea Exchange

Background

C6
AI Data Infrastructure & Orchestration
Theme B · Theme C Linkage

AI models are only as good as the data they are trained and operated on. Every enterprise AI deployment — whether at MSFT, GOOGL, Baidu, or Alibaba — requires data pipelines, governance frameworks, and orchestration platforms to manage, clean, and deploy training and inference data at scale. This cluster captures the software layer that sits between raw data and functional AI — an unglamorous but structurally necessary tier.

Why High-Potential

  • AI makes data infrastructure more valuable, not less: Agentic AI and LLM deployments require higher data quality, lower latency, and stronger governance than previous analytics workloads. Incumbent platforms with embedded data estate become essential.
  • Sticky recurring revenue: Data cloud and integration platforms have 90%+ net revenue retention — once a data pipeline is built on Snowflake or Informatica, migration costs are prohibitive.
  • Enterprise AI monetisation is now: Palantir grew revenue 56% in 2025 and guided 61% growth for 2026 — AI Platform (AIP) is not a concept, it is closing US$10M+ enterprise contracts at scale.
  • Government demand: Palantir's government segment (US Department of Defense, allied agencies) provides a countercyclical revenue floor unavailable to commercial SaaS peers.
